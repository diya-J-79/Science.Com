What Are Cells?

Cells are the basic building blocks of all living things. The human body is composed of trillions of cells. They provide structure for the body, take in nutrients from food, convert those nutrients into energy, and carry out specialized functions. Cells also contain the body's hereditary material and can make copies of themselves.



Structure Of A Cell

Every cell has a different structure to better serve their function ,but the main stucture in animals cells are:

  • A nuclues(the nucleus stores genetic material which tells each organelle what to do)
  • Cytoplasm(this is a jelly like substace which holds all the organelles in place.)
  • Mitochondria(also known as the powerhouse of the cell, as it gives energy to the cell.)
    And,
  • Vacuole(The storage house, which stores fats and other substances for later use.)

Plant and Animal Cells

There are two types of major cells, plant cells and animal cells which have a few similarities and differences.


Similarities
  • Nucleus
  • Cytoplasm
  • Mitochondria
  • Vacuole

Differences
  • Plant Cells have a Cell Wall
  • Plant Cells have Chloroplast
  • Plant Cells have one large Vacuole where Animal Cells have many small Vacuoles
