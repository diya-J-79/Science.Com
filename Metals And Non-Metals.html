Metals vs Non-Metals

Majority elements in the periodic table are metals. This includes alkali metals, transition metals, lanthanides, actinides and alkaline earth metals. Metals are separated by nonmetals on a periodic table through a zigzag line starting from carbon, till radon. The elements between the two are phosphorus, selenium and iodine. These elements and elements right to them in the periodic table are nonmetals. Elements present just to the left of the line are termed as semimetals or metalloids. These will have the combined properties of both metals and nonmetals.

Very few elements in the periodic table are non-metals. These are present on the right-hand side in the periodic table. Elements that come under non-metals are sulphur, carbon, all halogens, phosphorus, hydrogen, oxygen, selenium, nitrogen and noble gases. In the periodic table, non-metals are located left of the halogens and to the right of the metalloids. Since noble gases and halogens are also non-metals, these elements are often referred to as non-metals.



Properties of metals

Metals have some charecteristics that non-metals don't, which are:

  • Shiny (lustrous) in nature
  • Good conductor of electricity and heat
  • Density and melting points are high
  • Malleable
  • Ductile
  • Solid at room temperature (except for mercury)
  • Opaque

  • Eaily Corrodible
  • Can loose electrons
  • Very Reactive

Properties of non-metals

Non-Metals have some charecteristics that metals don't, which are:

  • Dull
  • Poor conductors of electricity and heat
  • Density and melting points are low
  • Brittle
  • Non-Ductile
  • Maybe solids, liquids or gases at room temperature
  • Can be Transparent,Translucent or Opaque

  • The number of electrons in the outer shell is generally 4-8
  • Easily gain or lose valence electrons
  • Great Oxidizing Agents

Metal Reactions with Oxygen, Water and Acid

General reaction of metals and acid:
Metal + Acid --> Metal Salt + hydrogen

General reaction of metals and water:
Metal + Water --> Metal Hydroxide + hydrogen

General Reaction of metals and oxygen:
Metal + Oxygen --> Metal Oxide
